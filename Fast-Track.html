💧 Hydration matters. Aim for at least 64–80 oz (2–2.4 L) daily. With electrolytes, your tolerance increases — some people thrive on 100 oz+. Electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) prevent headaches and muscle cramps during fasting and help your body actually absorb water effectively.
What's OK During a Fast
✓ Free pass
Water, sparkling water, plain ice
✓ Free pass
Black coffee, plain green/black tea
✓ Recommended
Electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium)
✓ OK
Himalayan pink salt in water
⚠ Use sparingly
Herbal teas — watch for flavors that spike cravings
⚠ Caution
Zero-cal sweeteners — may trigger insulin response
✗ Avoid
Flavored drinks — taste = cravings = harder fast
✗ Breaks fast
Anything with calories, cream, sugar, juice
💡 The flavor trap: The more flavorful something is, the more your brain wants more flavor. Keep drinks as plain as possible — your taste buds adapt fast and water starts tasting great within a day or two.
Your Mindset Anchors
🪨
Empty is progress. That hollow feeling in your stomach? That's your body switching fuel sources. It's working. You're winning right now.
⏱
Hunger only lasts ~2 hours. Ghrelin (the hunger hormone) comes in waves. Each wave peaks and disappears within about 2 hours. If you outlast it — you've beaten it. The next wave will be weaker.
🌬️
Feeling light is progress. As hours pass, that lightness isn't weakness — it's your body running lean and clean on fat. Athletes call this "metabolic freedom."
🔋
Want electrolytes. A pinch of salt, some potassium, and magnesium keep energy stable, stop headaches, and make fasting dramatically more comfortable. Don't skip them.
🧠
Mental clarity is coming. Around 16–24h, most people feel a wave of calm mental energy from ketones fueling the brain. If you feel foggy now, it passes — clarity is on the other side.
🏆
Every hour is a deposit. You don't have to commit to the whole fast. Just commit to the next hour. Stack hours. Each one is a deposit into your health account.
